Coffee Challenges

Each challenge winner (the first person to meet the requirements of the challenge) will be treated to a coffee (or tea) by me!
R Function for p-Value Vectors
Write an R function to obtain a vector of p-values for all of the group comparisons. In theory, this might fit the necessary dummy-variable regressions and collect the p-values. The output would be a vector of p-values that could then be used in the p.adjust() function. This function should be generalizable so that it works for all of our group comparison needs. The syntax needs to be well commented and follow the Data Camp Style Guidelines.
R Function for Writing a Vector of Contrasts
Write an R function that will take a vector of the groups (e.g., the output from levels()) and will produce the vector of all contrast strings needed to submit to the glht() function. This function should be generalizable so that it works for all of our group comparison needs. The syntax needs to be well commented and follow the Data Camp Style Guidelines.
Inbox Zero
Inbox Zero is a project by Merlin Mann to keep his inbox manageable. [Read more here] According to Mann, the zero is not a reference to the number of messages in an inbox; it is "the amount of time an employee's brain is in his inbox." His point being that both time and attention are finite, and when an inbox is confused with a "to-do" list, productivity suffers. The challenge is to follow Mann's advice and clear your inbox. Show that your email is organized and the inbox is at zero (or close to zero) emails for at least 3 days by taking a snapshot of your screen so that the day/time and inbox are clearly visible in the photo. Send three days worth of photos to my inbox (which I will quickly archive) to claim the coffee.
